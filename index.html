About Me

I am a graduate student in Mathematics at Brigham Young University, focusing on mathematical biology and computational neuroscience. My thesis research applies deep learning and dimensionality-reduction techniques to high-dimensional electrophysiological recordings from mouse brains — decoding genetic information from the rhythms of neural activity.

I also work as in intern at the U.S. Department of Justice building geospatial data-visualization tools. And I previously interned at FAST Enterprises designing audit workflow software, and at Fortem Technologies training AI-guided airspace security drones. I hold a Top Secret security clearance and graduated summa cum laude as valedictorian of BYU's College of Physical, Mathematical, and Computational Sciences.

Outside the lab I teach Calculus, tutor students in mathematics and test prep, and enjoy studying geography and sitting down at a piano in my free time.
